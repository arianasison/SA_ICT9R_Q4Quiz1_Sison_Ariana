Palawan

Palawan's recognition as the most exquisite island this year is unsurprising, given its pristine turquoise waters, towering limestone cliffs, and captivating lagoons. These are just a few of the island province's remarkable features. Palawan harbors terrestrial and aquatic natural reserves, with dolphins in close proximity, expansive marine gardens inhabited by huge clams, white sand beaches serving as nesting grounds for sea turtles, a diverse array of 600 butterfly species, and luxuriant palm forests reminiscent of a fantastical island paradise.

Vigan

Vigan is a historic city situated in the province of Ilocos Sur. The city is renowned for its well-preserved Spanish architecture from the sixteenth century and its delectable cuisine. Vigan serves as the administrative center of Ilocos Sur. The distance from Metro Manila is just 407 kilometers, requiring a seven-hour road journey. Furthermore, it is just a mere 138 kilometers from San Fernando City in La Union and 82 kilometers from Laoag, the capital city of Ilocos Norte. The name "Vigan" is derived from the tuber plant known as "Bigaa," which belongs to the Gabi family and is characterized as a large Taro plant, a type of root crop.








Banaue Rice Terraces

For many Filipinos, the Banaue Rice Terraces rank as the world's eighth most impressive natural wonder, and they are so designated as a Philippine national cultural treasure. It is likely that the terraces were cut by hand in the Ifugao highlands. In the hilly areas of Ifugao and northern Luzon Island, there are rice terraces that date back more than two thousand years.The original purpose of the terraces was to provide a more manageable growing environment for rice, as the terrain drops away at astonishing angles.

Siargao

Siargao has a diverse selection of activities and attractions, from surfing and beautiful beaches to island hopping and road journeys through untouched landscapes. Siargao is without a doubt a tropical adventurer's dream location, with emerald green seas, coconut palm trees, and some of the Philippines' most luxurious beach resorts. The greatest time to come is between March and September, when the island's breathtaking beauty is at its height.
